Frequently Asked Questions

Does the Word output preserve formatting? ▾
Yes — paragraphs, headings, line spacing and basic structure are preserved in the .docx output. Complex multi-column or heavily formatted layouts may simplify due to browser PDF extraction limits, but all text content will be intact and fully editable.
What is OCR Mode for? ▾
OCR (Optical Character Recognition) is for scanned PDFs — documents where pages are images rather than digital text. Tesseract.js renders each page and recognises the characters. It is slower than Standard mode but works on documents that cannot be read otherwise.
Are my files sent to a server? ▾
Never. All processing — PDF parsing, OCR, and .docx generation — happens 100% inside your browser using PDF.js and Tesseract.js. Your files are never uploaded anywhere.
What's the difference between .docx and .doc? ▾
.docx (Word 2007+) is the modern open XML format compatible with Microsoft Word, Google Docs, LibreOffice and all modern editors. .doc is the legacy binary format for older versions of Word. We recommend .docx for all modern use.
Can I convert specific pages only? ▾
Yes. Select "Custom Range" in the options and enter your pages separated by commas — for example 1-3, 5, 7-9. This is useful for large PDFs where you only need certain sections.
