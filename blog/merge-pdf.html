What is PDF Merging?

PDF merging combines multiple separate PDF files into one unified document. Instead of sending five attachments, you merge them into one clean file. Instead of managing dozens of separate invoices, you combine them into a single monthly archive.

FAQs

How many PDFs can I merge at once? ▼
There is no hard limit — you can merge as many PDFs as your browser's memory allows. Most users merge 2–20 files without any issues.
Will the bookmarks and links in the PDFs be preserved? ▼
Basic text content and page structure are preserved. Hyperlinks embedded in the PDF may or may not carry over depending on the complexity of the original files.
Can I merge password-protected PDFs? ▼
Password-protected PDFs need to be unlocked first. Use the PDF Unlock tool to remove the password, then merge.
What's the difference between merging and appending? ▼
Appending adds one PDF to the end of another (sequential). Merging can interleave pages from multiple PDFs in any order — which is what our Type 2 and Type 3 modes enable.
